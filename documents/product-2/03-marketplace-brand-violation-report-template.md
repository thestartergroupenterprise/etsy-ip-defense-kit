## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

Use this template to report trademark violations across major selling platforms. Each platform has its own IP reporting system. Fill in the [BRACKETED] fields with your specific information before submitting. Read the platform-specific instructions for each portal before filing — submitting an incomplete report is the most common reason reports are ignored.

**Before filing any report, confirm you have:**
- [ ] Evidence of your trademark rights (see Document 1 — Brand Rights Documentation Log)
- [ ] Screenshots of the infringing listing (with URL, price, and seller name visible)
- [ ] Screenshots of your own original brand use with dates
- [ ] The infringing listing URL(s) — copy and paste the exact URL from your browser

## PART C — PLATFORM-SPECIFIC SUBMISSION INSTRUCTIONS

### ETSY — Trademark Infringement Report

**Portal URL:** https://www.etsy.com/legal/ip/report

**Process:**
1. Go to the URL above while logged into your Etsy account
2. Select "Trademark" as the type of IP concern
3. Select your country and trademark registration status
4. If registered: enter your USPTO registration number
5. If unregistered: select "I have a common law trademark" and describe your use history
6. Enter the infringing shop name and listing URL(s)
7. Describe the infringement in the "Please explain" field — use the description from Part B above
8. Submit and save the case number Etsy provides

**What to enter in Etsy's "Please explain" field:**
"[YOUR BRAND NAME] is my [registered / common law] trademark in connection with [DESCRIBE YOUR PRODUCTS]. I have been using this mark commercially since [DATE]. The seller at [INFRINGING SHOP URL] is using my mark without authorization in their [shop name / product titles / product descriptions], which causes consumer confusion and infringes my trademark rights. I request immediate removal of the infringing content."

**Escalation path if no response within 7 days:** Email legal@etsy.com with your case number, subject line: "Trademark Report [YOUR CASE NUMBER] — Escalation Request"

---

### AMAZON — Trademark Infringement Report

**Portal URL:** https://www.amazon.com/report/infringement

**If you have Amazon Brand Registry:** Use the Brand Registry reporting tool at https://brandregistry.amazon.com — it has priority processing and additional enforcement tools.

**Process:**
1. Go to the URL above
2. Select "Trademark" under intellectual property type
3. Enter your trademark registration number (required for Amazon)
4. Enter the ASIN(s) of the infringing product(s)
5. Describe the infringement — use the description from Part B above
6. Submit

**Note:** Amazon gives significantly more weight to registered trademarks. If you have a pending application, you can still file but results may be slower. Joining Amazon Brand Registry provides stronger enforcement tools and requires a registered trademark.

**Escalation path:** Amazon Brand Registry support at https://brandregistry.amazon.com/branding/support or through the original report case page.

---

### TEMU — Trademark Infringement Report

**Portal URL:** https://www.temu.com/ip-report.html

**Process:**
1. Go to the URL above
2. Create an IP Rights Holder account if you don't have one
3. Submit your trademark documentation (registration certificate or evidence of use)
4. Once verified, report the infringing product by entering the URL
5. Temu requires advance registration as a rights holder before accepting reports

**Important:** Temu's process is slower than Etsy and Amazon. Set an expectation of 5 to 14 business days for initial response. Document all submissions carefully.

---

### ALIEXPRESS — Trademark Infringement Report

**Portal URL:** https://activities.aliexpress.com/en/ip_right_home.htm

**Process:**
1. Register as an IP rights holder on AliExpress IP Protection Platform
2. Submit trademark certificate or evidence of use for verification
3. Once account is verified, report infringing product URLs through the platform dashboard
4. AliExpress processes reports in batches — allow 7 to 10 business days

### INDEPENDENT WEBSITE / STANDALONE STORE

For brand violations on a seller's own website (not a marketplace):

**Step 1 — Identify the hosting provider**
Go to: https://who.is or https://www.whois.com
Enter the infringing domain name.
Under "Registrar" or "Name Servers," identify the hosting company.

**Step 2 — Find the hosting provider's DMCA / IP abuse contact**
Go to: https://www.whoismyhostingprovider.com (alternative lookup)
Or search: [HOSTING PROVIDER NAME] trademark abuse contact

**Step 3 — Send the report to the hosting provider**
Subject line: "Trademark Infringement Notice — [YOUR BRAND NAME] — [INFRINGING DOMAIN]"

Use this letter template:

---
Dear [HOSTING PROVIDER] Legal/Abuse Department,

I am writing to report trademark infringement by one of your customers.

**My trademark information:**
Brand name: [YOUR BRAND NAME]
Registration status: [Registered with USPTO, Registration #[NUMBER] / Common law trademark, in use since [DATE]]
Owner: [YOUR NAME OR BUSINESS NAME]

**Infringing content:**
Domain: [INFRINGING DOMAIN]
Infringing URL(s): [PASTE URL(S)]
Description of infringement: [DESCRIBE — e.g., "The site at [URL] is using my trademark [BRAND NAME] in its domain name and throughout its product pages without my authorization, causing consumer confusion."]

I request that you immediately remove or disable access to the infringing content.

I have a good faith belief that the use described above is not authorized by the trademark owner, its agent, or the law.

I declare under penalty of perjury that the information in this notice is accurate and that I am the owner (or authorized agent of the owner) of the trademark described above.

[YOUR SIGNATURE]
