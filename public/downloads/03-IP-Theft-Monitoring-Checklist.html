IP Theft Monitoring Checklist

ETSY IP DEFENSE KIT — Document 3 of 5 Professional IP Protection Templates for Etsy Sellers

HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

Run this checklist once per week for active shops, or once per month at minimum. The goal is to catch theft early — before the copycat builds sales momentum with your photos. Budget 30–45 minutes per monitoring session. Bookmark all the search links in this document. The faster you catch it, the faster you can file. Log every finding in your tracker table at the bottom.

BEFORE YOU BEGIN — PREPARE YOUR SEARCH ASSETS

Have these ready before each monitoring session:

  • ☐ Image files saved locally: Keep a folder of your 3–5 most distinctive product photos (the ones thieves most often steal) saved on your computer. You'll upload these to reverse image search tools.
  • ☐ Your exact listing titles: Copy your top 5–10 listing titles into a notes file. You'll search these verbatim.
  • ☐ Your most distinctive phrases: Any unique copy you wrote (product descriptions, taglines, shop announcements). Keep 2–3 ready to search.
  • ☐ Your shop name and variations: Including common misspellings or close variants.

PLATFORM 1: ETSY

Step 1.1 — Reverse Image Search for Your Photos

  • Go to Google Images: https://images.google.com
  • Click the camera icon (🔍 by image)
  • Upload your product photo
  • Click "Find image source" — review ALL results
  • Look for: Etsy listings that aren't yours, any e-commerce pages using your image
  • Repeat for your top 3 photos

Also try:

  • TinEye (often finds older stolen copies): https://tineye.com
  • Bing Visual Search: https://www.bing.com/visualsearch
  • Yandex Images (excellent for finding Asian marketplace copies): https://yandex.com/images — upload photo, click camera icon

    • Step 1.2 — Etsy Keyword Search

    • Go to https://www.etsy.com
    • Search your exact listing title (copy-paste, no modifications)
    • Sort by "Most Recent" — new copycat shops often appear at the bottom
    • Scroll through results: do any listings use your photos? Your exact copy?
    • Repeat with 2–3 variations of your title

    Step 1.3 — Etsy Title & Description Text Search

    • Take your most distinctive product description phrase (something uniquely worded)
    • Search it verbatim in Etsy search: wrap in quotes for exact match
    • If you find listings with your exact wording, click in and compare photos

    Step 1.4 — Direct Shop Monitor (for known copycats)

    If you've caught a specific seller before:

  • Visit their shop directly
  • Check if they've re-listed after removal
  • Check if they've opened a new shop with a slight name variation

    • PLATFORM 2: TEMU

    Step 2.1 — Image Search on Temu

    • Go to: https://www.temu.com
    • Click the camera icon in the search bar (top right)
    • Upload your product photo
    • Review all results for matching images

    Step 2.2 — Keyword Search on Temu

    • Search your product type + key descriptors (e.g., "handmade moon ring sterling silver")
    • Sort by "New Arrivals" — recently added copies often appear first
    • Hover/click any suspicious listings to check for your exact photos

    Step 2.3 — Yandex for Temu (Most Effective)

    Yandex catches Temu imagery better than Google for Chinese-sourced goods:

  • Go to: https://yandex.com/images
  • Upload your photo via the camera icon
  • Look specifically for temu.com results in the results page
  • Click "View all results" to see extended matches

    • PLATFORM 3: ALIEXPRESS

    Step 3.1 — AliExpress Visual Search

    • Go to: https://www.aliexpress.com
    • Click the camera icon in the search bar
    • Upload your product image
    • Review results carefully — these often show the original factory supplier, which can confirm where a Temu/Etsy thief is dropshipping from

    Step 3.2 — Keyword Search on AliExpress

    • Search your product category + materials/style
    • Filter by "Orders" (most ordered) — high-volume listings using your photos are a priority
    • Filter by "New" — catch new listings using stolen images

    Step 3.3 — Google Search: site:aliexpress.com

    • Go to Google
    • Search: site:aliexpress.com [YOUR PRODUCT DESCRIPTION]
    • Example: site:aliexpress.com handmade moon ring sterling silver oxidized
    • Review any close matches

    PLATFORM 4: PINTEREST

    Pinterest is frequently where stolen Etsy photos spread before showing up on other platforms.

    Step 4.1 — Pinterest Visual Search

    • Go to: https://www.pinterest.com
    • Upload your product photo via the search bar (click camera icon)
    • Look for pins using your image that you did NOT post

    Step 4.2 — Google Image Search for Pinterest

    • In Google Images, search your product keywords
    • Filter to only show Pinterest results by adding site:pinterest.com to the query
    • Look for your photos pinned to boards you don't own

    Step 4.3 — Check Who Repinned Your Images

    • Find your own pins (if you have a Pinterest account)
    • Click the three dots on your pin → "See who repinned"
    • Look for repins that link OUT to competitor shops instead of back to you

    PLATFORM 5: AMAZON

    Step 5.1 — Amazon Image Search

    • Go to: https://www.amazon.com
    • Click the camera icon in the search bar (top of app on mobile, or search bar on desktop)
    • Upload your product photo
    • Review results for your exact images

    Step 5.2 — Keyword Search on Amazon

    • Search your product title with key descriptors
    • Sort by "Newest Arrivals"
    • Look for: listings with your photos, listings with your exact description copy

    Step 5.3 — Google: site:amazon.com

    • Google: site:amazon.com "[YOUR UNIQUE PRODUCT PHRASE]"
    • If your exact description language appears in an Amazon listing, that's a strong signal of copy-paste theft

    ADVANCED SEARCH TECHNIQUES

    Exact Phrase Detection (any platform, via Google)

    Use Google's exact match search to find your unique copy anywhere on the web:

    "[paste your unique product description phrase here]"

    Example: "hand-forged sterling silver moon phase ring with oxidized finish"

    If your exact phrase appears on another shop or site, it's strong evidence of description theft.

    Image Metadata Check (for proving YOUR ownership)

    To verify your photo is the original:

  • Right-click your original photo → Properties / Get Info
  • Check "Date Created" — this is your proof of earlier creation
  • Alternatively, use: https://exifdata.com — upload your original file to extract embedded creation date
  • Screenshot and save this. It's evidence if you need to escalate.

    • WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU FIND THEFT

    If you find a stolen photo or copied listing:

    • Do NOT contact the platform first. Document everything first.
    • Screenshot the infringing listing — full URL visible in browser bar
    • Screenshot your original listing — with your URL visible
    • Log in your tracker (below)
    • Then act:
      • - Etsy → Use Document 1 (DMCA) + Document 4 (Filing Guide) - Non-Etsy → Use Document 1 (DMCA) for the appropriate platform - For direct seller contact → Use Document 2 (C&D Letter)

    Severity triage:

  • 🔴 HIGH: They are using your exact photos and/or selling a replica product → File DMCA + C&D simultaneously
  • 🟡 MEDIUM: They have similar photos but not identical → Document it, monitor, decide on DMCA
  • 🟢 LOW: Same product niche, different photos → Not infringement. Move on.

    MONITORING SCHEDULE TEMPLATE

    Set a recurring reminder (calendar, phone, task app) for:

    • Weekly sweep (high-priority items): Etsy keyword search + reverse image of top 3 photos
    • Monthly deep dive: All 5 platforms, all techniques above
    • After going viral (social share, press mention): Run a full sweep within 24 hours — viral moments spike theft
