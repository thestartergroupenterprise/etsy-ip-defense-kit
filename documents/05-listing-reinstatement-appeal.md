## HOW TO USE THIS TEMPLATE

This template is for a specific and infuriating scenario: a thief steals your photos, someone reports THEIR listing to Etsy using YOUR images as "evidence," and Etsy's automated system suspends YOUR listing — the original. Or: Etsy removes your listing after a copyright complaint you believe is fraudulent or made in error. Fill in every [BRACKETED] field. This appeal is sent via Etsy's case system or via email to copyright@etsy.com. Response time varies (3–10 business days). Stay factual, calm, and evidence-heavy. Emotional appeals have lower success rates than evidence-based ones.

## SENDING THIS APPEAL

**Primary method:**
1. Email to: copyright@etsy.com
2. Subject line: `Listing Reinstatement Request — [SHOP NAME] — Listing #[ID]`
3. Attach all evidence items
4. Send and screenshot the sent email

**Secondary method (if you have a case number):**
1. Log in to Etsy Help Center
2. Go to your open case
3. Reply to the case with this appeal text + attachments

**After sending:**
- Set a 3-day reminder to follow up if no response
- If no response after 7 days: resend with "FOLLOW-UP — Second Request" added to the subject line

## ESCALATION IF APPEAL IS DENIED

If Etsy denies your appeal and you believe the denial is in error:

1. **File again** with additional evidence (stronger is better — add more proof of your original creation date)
2. **Post in the Etsy Seller Community** (community.etsy.com) — document your situation factually. Other sellers and Etsy staff read these forums.
3. **Contact Etsy via social media** (Twitter/X: @etsy or @EtsySells) — public visibility sometimes accelerates response
4. **Consult an IP attorney** — if your shop generates significant revenue, a formal legal letter from an attorney often produces faster results than seller appeals
5. **File with the Better Business Bureau** — Etsy monitors BBB complaints and often responds to them
