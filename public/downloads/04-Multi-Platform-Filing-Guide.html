HOW TO USE THIS GUIDE

This is the step-by-step "no more spinning your wheels" guide. Each section gives you the exact URL, exact form fields, and what to paste where — for every major platform. Use this alongside Document 1 (DMCA Takedown Notice) as your source material. Read the platform section before you open the form so you know what's coming. Most platforms respond within 3–7 business days. File, log your case number, and move on.

PLATFORM 1: ETSY

The One Thing You Must Know First

Etsy has TWO different IP reporting paths. One works. One doesn't.

  • ❌ The broken path: Etsy Help Center → "Report a problem" → leads to a loop that goes nowhere
  • ✅ The working path: https://www.etsy.com/legal/ip/ — Etsy's official IP reporting portal

Always use: https://www.etsy.com/legal/ip/

Etsy Filing — Step by Step

Time required: 10–15 minutes per report

Step 1: Navigate to the IP portal Go to: https://www.etsy.com/legal/ip/ (Bookmark this. You'll use it every time.)

Step 2: Select your infringement type

  • For stolen photos → select "Copyright"
  • For someone copying your brand name or logo → select "Trademark"
  • For someone selling counterfeit goods branded as yours → select "Counterfeit"

    • This guide covers Copyright (most common for Etsy sellers).

    Step 3: Fill in the form fields

    Step 4: Submit Click Submit. You will see a confirmation page and receive a confirmation email with a case number within 24–48 hours.

    Step 5: Log your case number Write down: Case #, Date filed, Listing URL. Use the monitoring log in Document 3.

    Step 6: Follow up if no response in 7 days Email: copyright@etsy.com Subject: "Follow-up on DMCA Case #[YOUR CASE NUMBER]" Body: "I submitted a copyright infringement report on [DATE] regarding [INFRINGING URL]. My case number is [#]. I have not received an update. Please advise on the status of this case."

    What Happens Next (Etsy Timeline)

    • Day 1–2: Confirmation email with case number
    • Day 3–7: Etsy reviews. If valid, they send a takedown notice to the infringer.
    • The infringer has 10 days to file a counter-notice.
    • If no counter-notice: Etsy removes the content. You get a resolution email.
    • If counter-notice filed: Etsy notifies you. You then have 10 business days to file a court action or the content goes back up.
    • If your listing gets suspended instead (it happens — see Document 5 for the appeal template).

    Multiple Listings on Etsy

    You can file multiple infringing URLs in a single report — paste each URL on a separate line in the "Infringing content URL" field, or use the "Add another URL" button if the form provides one.

    If reporting against multiple different sellers, file a separate report for each seller.

    PLATFORM 2: TEMU

    The One Thing You Must Know First

    Temu's IP portal requires no account to file. However, your contact information must be accurate — they may need to reach you for follow-up, and false filings can result in your own account being flagged.

    Temu IP Protection Portal: https://www.temu.com/ip-protect.html

    PLATFORM 4: AMAZON

    The One Thing You Must Know First

    Amazon has two separate IP reporting paths depending on whether you are a registered brand:

    • If you have Amazon Brand Registry → use the Brand Registry reporting tool (faster)
    • If you do NOT have Brand Registry → use the standard IP infringement report form

    Both options are covered below.

    Amazon Filing — Step by Step (Without Brand Registry)

    Amazon IP Infringement Report Form: https://www.amazon.com/report/infringement

    Time required: 15–20 minutes

    Step 1: Navigate to the form Go to: https://www.amazon.com/report/infringement

    Step 2: Select your issue

  • Click "I have a copyright issue"
  • Select "Unauthorized use of photos, videos, or other copyright-protected content"

    • Step 3: Fill in the form

    FieldWhat to Enter Your name[YOUR FULL LEGAL NAME] Email[YOUR EMAIL ADDRESS] Copyright owner[YOUR NAME or BUSINESS NAME] Description of original work"Original product photography created by me on [DATE]. My original listing: [YOUR AMAZON OR ETSY LISTING URL]." Type of infringing content"Product listing images" ASIN(s) or URL(s) of infringing content[PASTE THE AMAZON LISTING ASIN or full URL] How is it infringing?"The product listing uses my original photographs without authorization. I have not licensed these images to the seller or to Amazon. The images are identical/substantially similar to my original copyrighted work."

    Step 4: Declaration and submit Check all required declaration boxes and submit.

    Amazon's response time: 3–5 business days for clear-cut copyright cases.

    Amazon Filing — Step by Step (With Brand Registry)

    If you have an Amazon Seller account with Brand Registry enrolled:

    • Log in to Amazon Seller Central: https://sellercentral.amazon.com
    • Go to: Brands → Report a Violation
    • Search for the infringing ASIN or paste the listing URL
    • Click "Report a violation" next to the listing
    • Select "Copyright" → "Product image or content"
    • Fill in the description and submit

    Brand Registry reports are reviewed faster (often 24–48 hours) and receive higher priority.

    AFTER FILING ON ANY PLATFORM

    The 7-Day Follow-Up Rule

    Set a reminder for 7 days after filing. If you haven't received a resolution, send a follow-up:

    Subject: Copyright Infringement Follow-up — [Platform] Case #[YOUR CASE NUMBER] — [DATE FILED]

    Body:

    I filed a copyright infringement report on [DATE] regarding [INFRINGING URL]. My case number is [CASE NUMBER]. The infringing content remains live as of today, [FOLLOW-UP DATE]. Please advise on the current status and expected resolution timeline.

    Escalation Path (if platform doesn't act within 14 days)

    • Re-file the report from scratch with additional evidence (new screenshots, side-by-side comparisons)
    • File with the platform's DMCA agent directly via email (see Document 1 for agent contacts)
    • Report the seller to the platform's Trust & Safety / Abuse team separately (this is a different queue from IP reports)
    • For Etsy: Post in the Etsy seller community forums documenting the issue — community visibility sometimes accelerates platform action
    • Consult an IP attorney if infringement is large-scale or causing significant revenue loss
